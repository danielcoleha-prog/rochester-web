01 Dry Land Training Center

Rowing Dry Land
Training Center

The 3,000 sq ft state-of-the-art dry land home of UR Women's Varsity Rowing and Men's Club team — housed at river level in Brooks Crossing Apartments at 1500 S. Plymouth Ave. Home to CRCA All-Americans, All-Liberty League, and ACRA All-Regional athletes since opening in January 2015.

27 Model D Ergometers + 6 Dynamic Ergometers
2 BikeErgs
6 Spin Bicycles + 4-seat Swingulator Rowing Simulator
Dual cable machine, TRX rack, squat rack, leg press
Olympic weight sets, dumbbells, kettlebells, medicine balls
Sound system + 2 televisions
On the Water
02 On the Water

Genesee Waterways
Center

Rochester rows on the Genesee River — one of the most scenic and accessible rowing venues in the Northeast. The Genesee Waterways Center provides dock access, storage, and support for competitive racing and training year-round.

Direct access to the Genesee River
Centrally located near campus

October 2025 · Boston, MA

Head of
the Charles

Two University of Rochester collegiate four boats finished in back-to-back positions, ranking 16th and 17th at the historic Head of the Charles Regatta along the Charles River on Sunday afternoon. Overall, 40 boats competed in the collegiate fours competition, with both of Rochester's entries finishing in the top half of the field.

Head of
the Genesee

In the premier race of the day, Rochester's varsity eight A entry finished in third place among eight teams in that competition. The Yellowjackets finished with a time of 18:51.396, ending behind two William Smith teams. Rochester also had a B boat entered in that race, with that group ending in eighth place (21:00.424).
