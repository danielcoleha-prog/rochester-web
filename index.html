01 Dry Land Training Center

Rowing Dry Land
Training Center

The 3,000 sq ft state-of-the-art dry land home of UR Women's Varsity Rowing and Men's Club team — housed at river level in Brooks Crossing Apartments at 1500 S. Plymouth Ave. Home to CRCA All-Americans, All-Liberty League, and ACRA All-Regional athletes since opening in January 2015.

2 BikeErgs
6 Spin Bicycles + 4-seat Swingulator Rowing Simulator

Genesee Waterways

Rochester rows on the Genesee River — one of the most scenic and accessible rowing venues in the Northeast. The Genesee Waterways Center provides dock access, storage, and support for competitive racing and training year-round.

2025 · Boston, MA

Head of
the Charles

Two University of Rochester collegiate four boats finished in back-to-back positions, ranking 16th and 17th at the historic Head of the Charles Regatta along the Charles River. Overall, 40 boats competed in the collegiate fours competition, with both of Rochester's entries finishing in the top half of the field, qualifying for HOCR 2026.

16th & 17th of 40 · Qualified for HOCR 2026
2026 · Saratoga, NY

New York
States

Rochester claimed a win in the petite final for the 1V8, earned second in the petite final for the 2V8, and also secured a victory in the pair and quad. This result for the 1V was the first time Rochester has defeated the past Liberty League Champions Ithaca in program history. Overall, it was a memorable day of racing.

1V8 Petite Final Win · First Win vs. Ithaca
2026 · Saratoga, NY

Liberty
Leagues

Throughout the entire year, our sights were focused on Liberty Leagues — we had a countdown on our athlete log to serve as a constant reminder of our end goal. Countless hours of hard work went into our performance at this event. With our 1V placing third and 2V placing second, we were very proud of this moment.

From the Team

Hear From
the Team.

Julia Tricomi Class of 2027 · IR & Spanish · Minor in Latin American Studies

The student comes first in "student-athlete" and coaches understand very well that academic commitments and dedication are the top priority for students. This prioritization of academics does not mean we don't pursue athletic excellence, but rather that we cultivate individuals who are well-rounded in all aspects of their lives, just as we want to be multifaceted and dynamic athletes.

The flexibility within the Rochester curriculum has allowed me to pursue the creative side of myself by majoring in Creative Writing alongside my pursuit of being a scientist. Along with this, most schools of our undergraduate size are liberal arts focused, so it's unique to find a school with such a STEM focus without being a large school. Additionally, rowing at Rochester opened the door for me to serve as VSAAC President and engage with NCAA initiatives.

Elizabeth Carleton Class of 2026 · Biology & Creative Writing · VSAAC President
