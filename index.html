Golden Hour
Julia Tricomi Junior · IR & Spanish · Minor in Latin American Studies
On Academics

The open curriculum frees students up to either dive deep into their area of academic interest or to explore multiple unrelated subjects they are curious about. This leads to a rich variety of courses offered that can satisfy just about any topic of interest. The medium size of the school (~6,000 undergrads) and the spread of students across many concentrations means a large percentage of classes have fewer than 30 students — allowing for strong student-professor relationships that enhance learning, foster career connections, and create real community.

On Rowing

The student comes first in "student-athlete," and our coaches understand that academic commitments are usually the top priority — even before the sport we love. I studied abroad for a semester, something mandatory for my major that I had wanted to do for a long time. Where other programs might have discouraged that, our coaches actively encouraged me to go, and were excited to help me get back into rowing when I returned. This prioritization of academics doesn't mean we don't pursue athletic excellence — it means we cultivate individuals who are well-rounded, multifaceted, and dynamic.

On Academics

It's a very academically competitive school, but the students foster a community. We work together on assignments and help each other out instead of competing against one another.

On Rowing

With the flexibility of D3 athletics, I can compete at a high level in both sports that I do — and I feel genuinely supported in that endeavor.

Rachel Ruminer Sophomore · Mechanical Engineering
Elizabeth Carleton Senior · Biology & Creative Writing · VSAAC President
On Academics

The Rochester Curriculum has allowed me to pursue the creative side of myself by majoring in Creative Writing alongside my pursuit of being a scientist. Most schools of our undergraduate size are liberal-arts focused, so it's unique to find a school with such a STEM focus without being large — and it's still a Tier 1 research institution.

On Rowing

Rowing at Rochester opened the door for me to lead beyond the boat — serving as VSAAC President and engaging with NCAA initiatives. The program builds athletes who lead in every space they enter.
